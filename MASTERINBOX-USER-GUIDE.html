01The big picture

The MasterInbox is one screen where every reply from every cold-outreach campaign lands — across both EmailBison and Instantly. Instead of switching between two different platforms to read and reply to leads, you do everything in one place: read the lead's message, reply to it, add a label, add them to a follow-up sequence, and move on.

Everything is tied together by the lead's client — the partner brokerage the original outreach campaign was running for (Front Range Collective, Brooklyn Group, SERHANT., and so on). That client tag is what powers the sidebar lists, the dashboard intros count, and the per-client narrowing of every view.

The two systems behind the scenes. Outreach goes out through EmailBison and Instantly. When a lead replies, that reply is automatically pulled into MasterInbox and tagged to the right client (matched from the campaign name). You never need to open EmailBison or Instantly to read a reply — but the picker on the right-hand side will still push leads back into those systems when you want to (e.g. to add them to a subsequence or a follow-up campaign).

02Three-pane layout

Once you're signed in, the screen is split into three columns:

  • Sidebar (left) — Workspace home, every client list, system folders.
  • Middle — The tab bar of views (All Email, Interested, Objection…), the filter bar, and either a list of threads or one open thread.
  • Prospect panel (right) — Everything we know about the lead and the campaign the reply belongs to, plus actions like "Add to subsequence".

The sidebar's width is draggable — grab the right edge and slide it wider if you'd like more room for long client names. Your width preference is remembered per browser.

03The left sidebar

Top to bottom, the sidebar contains:

Sales inbox header

The little 📈 mark at the top — purely the workspace name.

“All”

The default destination. Clicking 👁 All takes you to every open conversation across every client and every campaign.

Search lists

A search box that filters the client list directly below it. Useful when you've got 24+ clients and want to jump to a specific one quickly. Start typing and only matching lists stay visible.

Client lists

One row per active client (Brooklyn Group, BHGRE Basecamp, Front Range Collective, etc.). Clicking a list narrows the whole middle pane to threads that belong to that client.

Each row has a 3-dot menu (visible on hover, or always visible on the currently-selected list):

  • Edit — opens the “Update list item” dialog where you can change the list's name and pick a new emoji from the picker grid.
  • Delete — removes the list from the sidebar. Threads themselves stay intact and stay tagged to the client; only the sidebar shortcut goes away.
Client lists update themselves. You don't have to manually add threads to a client's list — the moment a new reply lands and gets matched to that client, it shows up under the right list automatically.

Create list item

The + Create list item button at the bottom of the list lets you add your own custom list (not tied to a client). You pick an emoji from the same grid and give it a name. These are useful for personal buckets like “Follow up next week.”

Reminders / Archive / Trash

Three system folders pinned at the bottom:

  • 🕒 Reminders — Threads you snoozed will land here when the reminder fires. See Section 19.
  • 📦 Archive — Threads you've finished with but want to keep. They're hidden from the main views but searchable.
  • 🗑 Trash — Threads you've moved to trash.

Workspace badge & Logout

The bottom strip shows the workspace name (BrokerStaffer) and the ↪ Logout button. Logout drops you back to the sign-in page.

04The view tabs at the top

Across the top of the middle pane is a row of tabs — each is a saved view that pre-filters the thread list.

The default tabs

One tab per AI-labeling category, plus the catch-all on the left:

  • All Email — Every open thread.
  • Interested — Leads who want to talk, scheduled, said yes.
  • Objection — Leads asking about splits, fees, Zillow, structure.
  • Keep Warm — Polite “maybe later” — circle back in a few months.
  • OOO — Out-of-office auto-replies.
  • Not Interested — Polite no, happy where they are.
  • Not Qualified — Commercial-only, retired, runs their own brokerage.
  • Hostile — Aggressive demands to stop.

The “N new” pill

Each tab shows a small blue pill — like 28 new or 99+ new — that counts how many threads in that view you haven't opened yet. The number disappears once you read everything; it caps at “99+ new”.

When you're inside a client list, the count narrows too: opening Interested while viewing the Brooklyn Group list shows only the Brooklyn Group × Interested unseen count, not the global figure.

The 3-dot menu on each tab

Hover any tab and a ⋯ menu appears with:

  • Rename — change the tab's display name.
  • Delete — remove the tab entirely.

The + button

At the end of the tab row, the + button lets you create your own custom view. You give it a name, optionally pick a label to filter by, and hit Save. It becomes a new tab.

05The Filters bar

Just below the tabs you'll see two buttons:

  • ↻ Refresh — re-loads the current view immediately.
  • Filters — opens a panel where you can compose ad-hoc filter rules.

What you can filter by

FieldWhat it matches

CampaignsThreads tied to specific EmailBison or Instantly campaigns.

  • Apply Filter — runs the filter immediately, just for this session. The URL updates so you can share the link.
  • Save as new view — turns the filter into a permanent tab at the top.
  • Save to <view> — if you're already inside a custom view, you can overwrite its saved filter with the current rows.

06The thread list

Below the filter bar is the list of conversations matching the current view / list / filter combo. Each row gives you the most important details at a glance:

  • Blue dot on the very left — the thread has a new reply you haven't opened yet. Goes away once you click in.
  • Checkbox — select rows for bulk actions (mark unread, archive, trash, add label, etc.).
  • Channel icon — small mail icon, indicating the inbound was email.
  • Sender — the lead's name, falling back to email address if the name isn't known.
  • Source badge — purple INSTANTLY or BISON chip telling you which outreach platform the reply came from.
  • Client chip — orange Front Range Collective chip showing which client this thread belongs to. “Unknown” clients (no match) show a muted gray chip.
  • Campaign chip — light-blue chip with the campaign name, truncated.
  • Label chips — every label the thread has been tagged with, whether by the AI labeler or by you.
  • Subject + preview — the email subject line, then the first ~120 characters of the latest reply.
  • Timestamp — relative on the day (“22:57”), absolute after (“20 May”).

Paging

If there are more than 100 threads in the view, you'll see pager arrows on the right of the list header (1–100 of 268 · Page 1 / 3). Use the < / > buttons to flip pages.

Bulk actions

Once you tick one or more rows, a toolbar above the list shows actions you can apply in batch: mark as read/unread, add a label, archive, move to trash, export. These work on every selected row at once.

07Reading a thread

Clicking a thread opens it in the middle pane. The top of the pane is a toolbar, then come the message cards.

Thread toolbar (top, left to right)

  • ← Back — return to the thread list.
  • ⌃ Previous / ⌄ Next — jump up/down the list without going back.
  • ↻ Refresh — re-pull the thread.
  • 🏷 Labels — pick / unpick labels for this conversation.
  • 🕒 Snooze — set a reminder. The thread vanishes from the inbox and re-appears in Reminders at the chosen time.
  • ✉ Mark unread — un-read the thread (puts the blue dot back).
  • 📦 Archive / Unarchive — move to the Archive folder, or restore.
  • 🗑 Trash / Restore — move to the Trash folder, or restore.

Message cards

Each message in the thread is a card, ordered oldest at the top → newest at the bottom. Inbound cards (from the lead) have a soft green tint and align to the left. Outbound cards (from you) align to the right with a blue avatar.

Each card shows the sender's email + name, the subject line, the time sent, and the body. Long bodies are collapsed with a “show more” caret you can expand.

On inbound cards only, the card header has three small action icons:

  • Reply — opens the composer with TO set to the lead and CC pre-filled from the sender of this specific message.
  • Reply all — same TO, but CC pre-filled from every participant who has been on the thread so far (excluding you and the lead).
  • Forward — opens the composer with a quoted block of this message and a blank TO field; you fill in who to forward to.

The floating Reply button

The big rounded Reply button at the bottom-right of the thread is shorthand for “Reply to the most recent inbound on this thread.” Same behavior as the Reply icon on the latest message card.

08Replying to a lead

The composer opens as a panel pinned to the bottom-right. From top to bottom:

FieldWhat it does
FromThe sending mailbox. Pinned to the same address the lead's reply came in on — you can't accidentally reply from the wrong mailbox.
ToThe lead's email. Pre-filled, can be edited.
Cc / BccPre-filled based on which Reply button you clicked (see Section 7). The Cc row auto-opens whenever there's anyone in it so you can see who's getting looped in before sending.
SubjectRead-only on replies. It always matches the subject of the message you're replying to — this is what keeps the recipient's Gmail threading the conversation correctly. On Forwards the subject is fully editable and pre-filled with Fwd:.
BodyPlain-text editor. Line breaks render as new lines on the recipient's side.

AI reply

The ✦ AI reply button in the composer footer asks the Reply Agent (see Section 16) to draft a response for you. The full conversation history goes into the request, so the draft references what's already been discussed. The draft drops straight into the body field — review, edit, send.

Attachments

The 📎 Attach file and 🖼 Attach image buttons let you add files up to 25 MB each, 50 MB combined. Attachments are only available on EmailBison threads — Instantly's reply endpoint doesn't accept attachments, so the buttons are hidden when the thread came in via Instantly.

Add signature

When a signature is configured on the sending mailbox, a small “Add signature of the email account” checkbox shows up between the body and the toolbar. Tick it and your saved signature gets appended on send.

Send

The blue Send button on the bottom-right pushes the reply through EmailBison or Instantly (whichever the thread came from) and records the outbound message on the thread.

09The Prospect panel

The right-hand column shows everything we know about the lead and the campaign they're in. From top to bottom:

Header

Avatar (initials), the lead's full name, their email — with copy-to-clipboard icons on both. Below that, any labels the thread has are rendered as colored chips.

Tabs

  • Details — the default view (the rest of this section).
  • Attachments — files included on this conversation.
  • Notes — your own scratch notes about the lead.

Campaign section

SourceInstantly or EmailBison, depending on where the outreach went out from.
ClientWhich BrokerStaffer client this campaign belongs to (matched from the campaign name).
CampaignThe full campaign name on the source platform.

And then one action button — see Sections 10 & 11 for what each does:

  • Add to subsequence — only on Instantly threads.
  • Add to follow-up campaign — only on EmailBison threads.

About section

Email, email domain, title, company. Any extra fields the source platform shared (job title, location, license number, agency, etc.) show up here too.

Socials section

If the lead has a LinkedIn URL, Twitter / X, Facebook, Instagram, or website on file from the source, each appears as a clickable row that opens in a new tab.

Custom section

Anything the outreach platform sent that isn't in the structured fields above lands here — useful for per-campaign enrichment data (firstName, LicenseNumber, AgencyZip, etc.).

10Subsequences (Instantly)

In Instantly, a subsequence is a branch off the main campaign — a separate sequence of follow-up emails you've pre-built (e.g. "Interested Leads Nurture", "OpsLabs Interested Leads") and want to drop specific leads into.

How to add a lead

  1. Open the lead's thread.
  2. In the Prospect panel on the right, find the Campaign section.
  3. Click Add to subsequence. A dropdown shows every subsequence attached to this thread's campaign.
  4. Pick one. A confirmation toast appears.

Behind the scenes, MasterInbox finds the lead's Instantly ID by their email, then asks Instantly to move them into that subsequence. The lead is added immediately — Instantly takes a few seconds to start scheduling the subsequence's emails.

Only subsequences from the same campaign show up. If your campaign has no subsequences attached, the dropdown will say so. Create the subsequence inside Instantly first, then it'll appear here.

11Follow-up campaigns (EmailBison)

EmailBison's equivalent of a subsequence is a "reply followup campaign" — a separately-configured campaign with type reply_followup that resumes the conversation on the same email thread.

How to add a lead

  1. Open the lead's thread (must be from EmailBison).
  2. In the Prospect panel, click Add to follow-up campaign.
  3. The dropdown lists every reply followup campaign in the EmailBison team this thread belongs to.
  4. Pick one. The lead — plus the latest reply on the thread — gets pushed into that campaign. EmailBison resumes follow-up sends from the most recent reply in the same email thread.
EmailBison vs Instantly. The mechanics are similar but the underlying systems are different. On Instantly the lead moves into a sub-sequence of the original campaign. On EmailBison the lead (and their thread) are pushed into a separate campaign that's been marked as "reply followup".

12How the inbox stays up to date

You don't have to hit refresh. The inbox stays fresh in two complementary ways:

  • Realtime push — every new reply, new thread, and new label change is delivered to your browser within a second or two of the event happening.
  • Background poll — every 15 seconds, the inbox also re-pulls the current view as a safety net. The poll automatically pauses while the browser tab is hidden so it never burns CPU in the background.

If you ever want to force an update right now, the ↻ refresh button in the filter bar (or in the thread toolbar) gives you that — but it shouldn't normally be needed.

13Settings · Labels

The Labels page is where you manage the chips that get attached to threads — by you, by bulk actions, and by the AI labeler.

Each label has

NameThe displayed text, e.g. "Interested", "Hostile".
ColorOne of green / red / amber / zinc / stone / pink / blue. Controls the chip color across the app.
SentimentPositive / Negative / Neutral. Used for dashboard roll-ups and which icon a tab gets.
PlatformEmail-only or both. Restricts where the label can be applied.
ObligationFlag for labels that imply a to-do (e.g. "Meeting Booked").

What you can do

  • Click any label row to edit name, color, sentiment.
  • Drag-handle to reorder — top-to-bottom controls the order on chips and dropdowns.
  • Create new labels with the + button. They appear in the filter builder, the bulk-action menu, and the AI labeler's category options immediately.
  • Delete labels you no longer need — the chip is removed from every thread it was on.

14Settings · Clients

The Clients page is the catalog of BrokerStaffer clients we're running outreach for. The sidebar lists, the dashboard intros count, and every "Client" chip on the thread list are all driven by what's here.

Each client has

NameThe canonical display name shown everywhere (e.g. "Front Range Collective").
AliasesA list of additional strings to look for when matching campaign names. If a campaign is named "FRC - Denver Spring 2026", adding FRC as an alias on Front Range Collective will route those threads to the right client.
Thread countHow many threads currently point at this client.

How matching works

When a new reply lands, MasterInbox looks at the campaign name and scans for the client whose name or any alias appears in it. The longest match wins — so "Howard Hanna NYC" beats "Howard Hanna" if both apply, because it's more specific.

Add an alias, backfill happens automatically. When you add a new alias, MasterInbox immediately re-checks every thread currently in the "Unknown" bucket and re-tags any that now match. You don't have to do anything extra.

The "Unknown" client

There's a built-in fallback called Unknown. Any reply whose campaign name doesn't match any client lands here. You can browse the Unknown bucket like any other client list, figure out which alias would have matched, add it, and the threads will move.

15Settings · AI Labeling

The AI Labeling page controls the automatic tagging of incoming replies. Every time a new lead reply lands, MasterInbox can ask an AI model to read it and pick the best label (Interested, Objection, Hostile, etc.).

Settings

EnabledMaster on/off switch. When off, replies still come in — they just don't get auto-labeled.
ProviderOpenAI by default. You enter an API key once; it's encrypted at rest.
ModelWhich model to use. gpt-4o-mini is the default — fast and inexpensive, plenty smart for this task.
Label old repliesIf on, the next labeling run will also process existing un-labeled threads (a backfill pass).
Re-label ongoingIf on, when an additional reply arrives on a thread that already has a label, the model re-evaluates and may swap.
Custom promptThe instructions sent to the AI. The default is tuned for the BrokerStaffer / Zillow-Preferred-Brokerage context, with explicit rules for objections, "later" answers, and aggressive removal requests.
Category setWhich labels the model is allowed to pick from. Restricting this is how you stop the model from inventing labels you don't have.

"Run" button

The Run button on this page triggers an explicit backfill — it walks every open thread, asks the AI to label the latest inbound on each, and applies the result. Useful right after you've added or renamed labels.

16Settings · Reply Agents

A Reply Agent is what powers the AI reply button in the composer. It generates a draft reply tailored to the conversation so you can review and send instead of writing from scratch.

Each agent has

NameInternal label.
ModeHuman-in-loop (drafts that you review — the safer default) or Auto (drafts auto-send when triggered, used carefully).
ToneFree-text tone hint passed to the model ("professional", "warm", "concise"…).
Response lengthShort / Medium / Long / Variable. "Variable" mirrors the lead's message length.
Max tokensA ceiling on draft length. Not a target — longer drafts need a longer response_length setting, not a bigger token budget.
TemperatureCreativity dial. 0.4 is a balanced default.
Provider & ModelOpenAI / Anthropic / OpenRouter / vLLM, and the specific model under it.
API keyEncrypted per-agent.
System promptThe role + rules the model follows. Includes explicit guidance for handling Interested, Objection, Keep Warm, Not Qualified and Hostile categories.
Channel filterEmail only / All. Limits which threads the agent will draft for.
Channel scopeYou can pin an agent to specific sending mailboxes if you want different agents per mailbox.
ActiveMaster on/off for this agent.
Auto-respond to new threadsWhen on, the agent immediately drafts a reply the moment a thread first arrives (still requires your click to send if mode is human-in-loop).

What goes into a draft

When you click AI reply, the agent receives:

  • The full conversation history — every message on the thread, inbound and outbound, oldest to newest, with timestamps. The model can reference earlier turns rather than re-pitch what's already been said.
  • The lead's name, your name (the sending mailbox), and the subject.
  • Your tone and response-length settings.
  • Your custom system prompt.

The draft appears in the composer body field with a yellow banner saying "Draft by <Agent name> — review before sending." A "Clear & write from scratch" link lets you discard the draft.

17Settings · Webhooks

The Webhooks page is the bootstrap utility that registers MasterInbox as a listener with both EmailBison and Instantly. You typically run this once during onboarding and again if the EmailBison teams change.

The page shows:

  • The webhook URLs MasterInbox is expecting events at.
  • A summary of which EmailBison teams have a registered webhook, and the registration status of the Instantly webhook.
  • A button to (re-)register — useful after a teammate creates a new EmailBison team or you rotate API keys.

18Settings · Members & Personal

Members

The Members page lists everyone with access to the BrokerStaffer workspace. From here you can invite a new user by email, manage roles, and remove members. A new invitee gets an email with a one-tap link to set their password and sign in.

Personal

The Personal page is where you change your own password and (if added in future) personal preferences. Currently it contains:

  • Email (read-only).
  • Change password — current password + new password + confirmation.

19Reminders, Archive, Trash

Reminders

The 🕒 Snooze button on a thread (in the toolbar) lets you pick "later today", "tomorrow morning", "next week", or a custom date and time. The thread vanishes from the inbox and re-surfaces in Reminders when the time hits. The blue dot returns so it stands out.

Archive

The 📦 Archive button on a thread removes it from active views without deleting anything. Archived threads are still searchable and you can restore them with the same button (it becomes "Unarchive" while you're on an archived thread).

Trash

The 🗑 Trash button moves a thread to the Trash folder. Same idea as Archive but reserved for stuff you want out of your way for good. You can still restore from Trash if you change your mind — nothing is hard-deleted.

20The top search bar

The full-width search bar at the top of the inbox runs across every thread in the workspace — open, archived, trashed, all of them. It looks across:

  • Sender / lead name
  • Lead email
  • Company
  • Subject line

Type a few characters and matching threads start appearing below the input as a dropdown — you can press Enter to open a full results page, or just click a result to jump straight to that thread. The keyboard shortcut is ⌘ K (Mac) or Ctrl K (Windows).

21Update: how Intros are counted

Until today, the "Intros" number on the dashboard was pulled from MasterInbox only — whenever a lead got tagged with the "Introduction" label there, it counted as one intro for that client.

Going forward, intros now come from two places:

  • MasterInbox — same as before, no change.
  • BrokerStaffer — a new source. Whenever a lead in the new BrokerStaffer app gets the "Introduction" tag, that also counts as an intro for the matching client.

The dashboard now shows the total of both as a single "Intros" number per client. So if MasterInbox has 3 intros for a client this week and BrokerStaffer has 2, the dashboard will simply show 5.

The "Last Intro" column also looks at both sources and shows whichever one happened most recently.

Behind the scenes, the dashboard pulls fresh data from both sources every 15 minutes automatically — or instantly when you click the refresh button (↻) at the top right. Clients are matched between the two systems by exact client name, so if a client's name on BrokerStaffer doesn't exactly match the name on the dashboard, their BrokerStaffer intros won't show up (we have a way to spot those mismatches if it ever happens).

Everything else on the dashboard — weekly targets, conversion rate, status colors, the summary card at the top — works exactly the same as before, just with the combined number powering it now.
