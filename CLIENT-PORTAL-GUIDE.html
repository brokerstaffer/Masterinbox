Section 01

1Welcome & access

Your portal is a live window into the candidates Broker Staffer is introducing to your brokerage — plus the lists that keep your team safe from accidental cold outreach.

What the portal is for

Every time we introduce a new recruiting candidate to your brokerage, it shows up here. You move them through your hiring stages, keep notes alongside the lead, and tell us when you need a replacement. You also maintain three lists that protect your people and reputation: Your Agents, DNC List, and Team — anyone on those lists is automatically excluded from any of our campaigns, immediately.

How to open it

Your portal lives at a private URL we sent you. Anyone on your team with that URL can view and edit. No password, no login screen, no separate account to set up. Bookmark it, share it with your hiring team, and you're done.

Treat the URL like a password

The link itself is the credential. Anyone who has it can see your pipeline and edit your lists. Share it with people you trust, and let us know any time you'd like a fresh URL generated.

What the sidebar shows

The four sections live in a sticky sidebar on the left:

  • Recruiting Pipeline — every introduction we've sent you, with stage tracking.
  • Your Agents — your brokerage's own real estate agents (auto-protected from outreach).
  • DNC List — agents and companies we should never contact.
  • Team — your internal team members (also auto-protected).

Each item shows a live count next to its name so you always know how many records are in each list at a glance.

Section 03

3Stages explained

Every candidate starts as an Introduction. You move them forward (or sideways) by clicking the stage chip on their row. There are seven stages, each colour-coded so you can scan the pipeline at a glance.

Introduction Phone Screen Interview Hired Keep Warm We/They Rejected No Show / No Response
StageUse it when…
Introduction The default. Every new candidate lands here when Broker Staffer introduces them to you. They're a warm lead, ready for your first outreach.
Phone Screen You've made initial contact — a brief call, voicemail, or text to gauge fit and interest. Move them here as soon as that first conversation starts.
Interview A full interview is scheduled or completed. This is where serious candidates live while you make a decision.
Hired Congratulations — they've joined your brokerage. Conversion metrics at the top of the page automatically pick this up.
Keep Warm A strong candidate but the timing isn't right — for them or for you. Keep them visible without losing context, ready to reactivate later.
We/They Rejected One side has passed. The candidate didn't fit, or they weren't interested in moving. Marks the candidate as closed but keeps the record so you don't get re-introduced to the same person later.
No Show / No Response The candidate ghosted — didn't pick up, didn't reply, didn't show. Use this when you've tried to follow up and got nothing. Different from "rejected" because no real signal either way.

Every field, automatically

We don't pick and choose which fields show up. If our data provider returns a field for an agent — even one we haven't seen before — it appears here, with a clean label, in the same grid. You'll never have to ask us to "expose" a field.

Click-to-act on contact details

Emails open your default mail app. Phone numbers dial directly on mobile. Websites and LinkedIn profiles open in a new tab. You don't have to copy / paste — just click.

Missing fields

When a field shows a dash (—) or doesn't appear at all, it's because our data provider didn't have that piece for that particular agent. It's not hidden or filtered — it just doesn't exist for that person yet. Agents are continuously re-enriched as new data arrives.

Section 05

5Your Agents

A protected list of every real estate agent who works for your brokerage. The moment you add someone here, their email is excluded from every Broker Staffer campaign — so we never accidentally cold-outreach to your own people.

Two ways to add agents

Use the Add agent button to add a single agent at a time (name, email, phone, license number, market). Or — much faster for a full roster — click Import CSV, drop in a spreadsheet, and we'll preview the first 50 rows before you confirm the import. The CSV recogniser accepts the obvious column names plus common synonyms (full_name, email_address, cell, lic, etc.), so you don't need to reshape your data.

The Status pill — what it means

Protected Pending Push failed No email
  • Protected — the agent's email is on our outreach exclusion list. Cold-outreach systems will skip them.
  • Pending — the exclusion is being pushed (rare and fleeting).
  • Push failed — one of the outreach systems briefly rejected the push. We retry automatically; if you ever see this stuck, ping us.
  • No email — the row has no email address, so there's nothing to exclude. The record is still saved.

The summary tiles

Two tiles at the top tell you exactly where you stand: Protected agents shows the total count of everyone in the list, and Added this month shows how many you've grown it by in the current month.

When to use Your Agents vs. DNC

Your Agents is for people who work at your brokerage today. DNC List is for outside agents and firms you don't want us approaching at all (former hires, competitors' agents you have a no-poach with, etc.). The effect is the same — they're excluded from campaigns — but the two lists keep your data organised.

Section 06

6DNC List

Your "Do Not Contact" list — for anyone outside your brokerage we should never reach out to. Adding an entry with an email immediately blocks that email across both of our outreach systems.

Two kinds of entries

  • Agents — individual real estate agents you don't want approached (former hires, agents who've already passed, agents under no-poach agreements, anyone you have history with).
  • Companies — entire brokerages to avoid. Useful when you have a relationship with the leadership of another firm and you don't want any of their people contacted.

Adding entries

Use Add to DNC for single entries — toggle between Agent / Company, give it a name, and optionally an email, phone, brokerage, and a note explaining why. The email is the key field: if you provide one, it goes onto the outreach exclusion list within seconds.

Or use Import CSV to bulk-load entries. The importer recognises a kind column (set it to agent or company — anything else defaults to agent) plus the same name / email / phone / brokerage / notes columns the manual form has. You'll see a preview of the first 50 rows before anything is committed.

The Status pill

Blocked Pending Push failed No email
  • Blocked — the email is on at least one outreach exclusion list. Outreach will not happen.
  • Pending / Push failed / No email — same meanings as on the Your Agents page.

Entries that get added automatically

Some DNC entries are created by our systems without you doing anything:

  • If a recipient asks to unsubscribe or stop, they're added to your DNC automatically and their email is excluded going forward.
  • If our AI flags a reply as hostile or threatening, the lead is automatically marked DNC and removed from the active funnel.

You don't need to manage these — they're a safety net so a hostile or unsubscribed recipient never gets contacted by us again.

Section 07

7Team

The internal users at your brokerage — owners, recruiting managers, anyone with a brokerage email address. Adding someone here protects their email the same way Your Agents does, with one extra layer of organisation: this list is for your office, not your active agents.

Adding a team member

Use Add member and provide three things: their full name, their email, and (optionally) their title (e.g. "Recruiting Manager", "Broker Owner"). That's it — Save, and their email is immediately excluded from outreach.

The Status pill

Protected Pending Push failed

Same meaning as on the other two protection lists. Every team member who has been successfully pushed to the exclusion lists shows the green Protected badge.

The Active switch

Use the per-row Active switch to deactivate a member without removing them. Useful for "this person used to be on our team but has moved on" — you keep the record but downgrade the row. Removing entirely uses the trash icon.
