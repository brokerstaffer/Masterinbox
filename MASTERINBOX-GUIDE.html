01What Master Inbox is

Master Inbox is your unified reply center. Every time a prospect responds to one of your cold-outreach campaigns, the message lands here — categorized by an AI, tied to the right lead record, and ready for your team to act on.

Instead of jumping between sending tools to triage replies, your sales team works from one screen: read the reply, see the prospect's full context on the right, send a response (often pre-drafted by your Reply Agent), and move on. Threads can be snoozed to come back later, archived when handled, or filtered into custom lists so each person sees only what's relevant to them.

The promise: Cut reply triage time in half, never miss a follow-up, and keep every conversation organized the way your team actually works.

02Signing in

Open the Corofy Master Inbox URL provided by your admin and sign in with your email and password.

If you've been invited but haven't set a password yet, you'll receive an invitation email with a link to set yours.

03The sidebar

The left rail is fixed and gives you one-click access to every part of Master Inbox.

Sales Inbox

  • All — every conversation in this workspace.
  • Your custom lists — saved filter views you or your team have created. They appear here automatically once saved.
  • + — create a new list straight from the sidebar.

Folders

  • Reminders — threads you've snoozed, waiting to come back.
  • Archive — threads you've finished with but want to keep.
  • Trash — threads you've removed.

Workspace switcher

At the very bottom of the sidebar. Shows the workspace you're currently in and how many you can access. Click it to switch.

Logout

One click below the workspace switcher.

04Workspaces & the Sync button

A workspace represents one team or one customer account — its own sending accounts, its own conversations, its own labels, AI configuration, and reply agents. Everything you see on screen at any moment belongs to exactly one workspace.

Switching

Click the workspace name at the bottom of the sidebar. A dialog opens with a search box and the full list of workspaces you belong to. Your current one is marked with a checkmark. Click any other workspace to switch — the entire interface refreshes to show that workspace's data.

The Sync button

When you add a new workspace inside EmailBison, Master Inbox doesn't know about it until you tell it to look. Open the workspace switcher and click Sync. Master Inbox will:

  • Pull in any newly-created workspaces from EmailBison.
  • Automatically register the inbound webhook for them, so replies start flowing immediately — no manual setup required.
  • Add you as an owner of each new workspace.
Safe to click any time. Existing workspaces are never touched. Their data, webhook setup, labels, and AI settings stay exactly as they were. Running Sync when nothing has changed simply tells you you're up to date.

05The inbox view

Open All or any custom list and you'll see a list of conversation rows, with the most recent at the top.

What each row shows

  • Sender name and company.
  • Any labels — small colored chips like Interested or Wrong Person.
  • Subject and the first line of the latest message.
  • Timestamp of the last activity.

The toolbar at the top

  • Search across all conversations.
  • Counts: "1–25 of 101" tells you which page you're on and how many threads match.
  • Pagination arrows for moving between pages.

Bulk actions

Select multiple rows by checking the boxes on the left, then apply labels, archive, mark unread, or move to trash on all of them at once.

06Opening a conversation

Click any row to open the full thread. You'll see three areas: a toolbar across the top, the message timeline in the middle, and the prospect panel on the right.

The thread toolbar

Back arrow
Return to the list you were viewing.
Prev / Next
Jump to the previous or next conversation in the list without leaving the thread view.
Label picker
Add or remove labels — both your team's manual labels and AI suggestions appear here.
Snooze (clock)
Push the thread out of the inbox until a chosen time. Becomes amber when the thread is currently snoozed.
Mark unread
Flag the thread so it stands out again as needing attention.
Refresh
Pull the very latest messages from EmailBison.
Archive
Clean it out of the inbox without deleting it. When you're already viewing the Archive folder, this same icon un-archives the thread.
Delete
Move the thread to Trash. From inside Trash, the same icon restores it.

The message timeline

Every message in the conversation appears in chronological order, oldest at the top. Long quoted reply chains are collapsed automatically — click the small caret to expand. Attachments are listed inline beneath each message.

07Replying to a prospect

The reply composer sits at the bottom of the thread.

  • From — choose which sending account to reply from. Useful when your team operates several sender domains.
  • Insert variables — drop in fields like the prospect's first name or company. Handy when responding to similar replies at scale.
  • Formatting — bold, italics, lists, links.
  • Attachments — drag and drop a file or click the paperclip.
  • Send — fires through EmailBison. The outbound message is recorded in the thread immediately.

AI-drafted replies

If a Reply Agent is configured and active, every inbound reply triggers it to write a draft. When you open the thread, the draft is already loaded in the composer — you review, optionally tweak, and click Send. In auto mode, the agent sends the draft directly without your review.

08The prospect panel

To the right of every thread, you'll find the prospect panel — the lead's full context, right where you need it. It has three tabs:

Details
Labels on this thread, contact info, company, role, location, social profiles, and any custom fields enriched on the lead record.
Attachments
Every file ever exchanged on this thread, in one place.
Notes
Internal notes only visible to your team. Use these for context that shouldn't leak into the reply itself.

09Labels

Labels are how Master Inbox organizes conversations. Each label has a color and meaning — Interested, Wrong Person, Meeting Booked, Out of Office, Cold Lead, and so on. They can be applied manually by your team or automatically by the AI labeler.

Managing labels

Open Settings → Labels. From there you can:

  • Create new labels with custom names, colors, and sentiments (positive / negative / neutral).
  • Mark a label as an Obligation — meaning a thread with that label is expected to be replied to.
  • Reorder labels by dragging.
  • Delete labels you no longer use.

Defaults

Each new workspace starts with a sensible set of labels — Interested, Not Interested, Not Right Now, Wrong Person, Do Not Contact, OOO Sequence, Automated Response, Add to Blocklist, Information Request, Meetings Booked, Cold-Leads, Form, and Unable to Categorize. You can rename or remove any of them.

10Filters & custom views

Above the thread list is the filter builder. Stack conditions to define exactly which conversations you want to see, then save the result as a list anyone on your team can reopen later.

Conditions you can combine

Labels
Show threads that have, or don't have, one or more chosen labels.
Channels
Restrict to specific sending accounts.
Reply Since
e.g., "our last reply was more than 3 days ago, less than 5 days ago" — perfect for staged follow-ups.
Last Message From
The most recent message in the thread came from us, or from them.
Message Counts
Number of times sent or received on this thread.

Click Save, give the list a name, and it joins the sidebar so your whole team can use the same view.

Example: A "Follow-Up Day 3" list might be — Labels NOT [Cold Leads, Wrong Person, Not Right Now] · Reply Since "more than 3 days, less than 5 days" · Last Message From "Me" · Sent count "1". Anyone who opens that list tomorrow sees exactly the threads ready for their second touch.

11Snoozing & reminders

Reminders let you push a thread out of sight until you actually need to think about it again — without losing track of it.

How to snooze

  1. Open any conversation.
  2. Click the clock icon in the toolbar.
  3. Pick a preset or choose a custom date and time.

Available presets

  • Later today (in 3 hours)
  • Tomorrow morning at 9:00 AM
  • This weekend, Saturday at 9:00 AM
  • Next Monday at 9:00 AM
  • Next week (in 7 days)
  • Custom — pick any date and time

The thread disappears from your inbox and lands under Reminders in the sidebar. The clock icon on the thread itself turns amber to show it's snoozed.

How reminders return to your inbox

Open the Reminders page. Any reminder whose scheduled time has passed is immediately released — the thread returns to your inbox and the reminder is marked done. Think of opening Reminders as checking your reminder queue.

Un-snoozing early

  • From Reminders, click the X next to a row.
  • Or open the thread itself and click the amber clock → Move back to inbox.

Peeking at a snoozed thread without un-snoozing

From Reminders, click the arrow on the right side of a row. The thread opens for reading — it stays snoozed until its scheduled time.

12Archive vs. Trash

Both Archive and Trash move a thread out of your inbox, but they mean different things:

Archive
"I'm done with this conversation, but I want to keep it around for reference." Think of it the way you'd archive an email in Gmail.
Trash
"I want this out of the way." Threads stay recoverable from Trash; nothing is permanently deleted unless you choose to.

Both views are accessible from the sidebar. While viewing either view, the matching icon in the thread toolbar restores the thread back to your inbox.

13AI Labeling

Every inbound reply runs through your AI labeler the moment it arrives. The AI assigns one or more labels — Interested, Wrong Person, Out of Office, and so on — based on the meaning of the message and the categories you've defined.

Configuring it

Open Settings → AI Labeling. You control:

  • Provider — choose your AI provider (OpenAI, OpenRouter, and others).
  • Model — pick the model you want to use.
  • API key — securely stored, never visible to anyone else.
  • Label old replies — one-time backfill onto every existing thread.
  • Relabel ongoing — re-evaluate labels each time a thread gets a new message.
  • Custom prompt — tell the AI exactly how your team thinks about each category.
  • Category set — the list of labels the AI is allowed to assign.

Once configured, this happens automatically. New inbound replies arrive in your inbox already labeled, usually within a few seconds.

14Reply Agents

Reply Agents are AI assistants that draft responses for your team. They read every inbound reply, understand the context, look at the lead's information, and either pre-write a draft (for your review) or send a reply directly.

Configuring an agent

Open Settings → Reply Agents. For each agent, you can set:

  • Name — internal label so your team knows which agent does what.
  • Mode — Human-in-the-Loop (AI drafts, human approves) or Auto (AI sends directly).
  • Channels — which sending accounts the agent operates on.
  • Tone — formal, friendly, casual, blunt.
  • Response length — short, medium, or long.
  • Max tokens — caps how long any response can run.
  • Provider, model, API key, temperature — full AI configuration per agent.
  • Custom system prompt — your sales messaging, talk tracks, do's & don'ts. Drop them in and the agent follows them.
  • Insert Variable — reference lead data inline in the prompt itself.

The Reply Agents dashboard

At the top of the Reply Agents page you'll find live stats: Total Agents, Active, Replies generated, Sent, Pending review, and tokens consumed (prompt, completion, total). It gives you an at-a-glance view of how much work the AI is doing for your team.

How drafts surface to your team

In Human-in-the-Loop mode, every new inbound reply triggers the agent within a minute or so. When a team member opens the thread, the draft is already in the composer ready to edit and send. In Auto mode, the agent sends the draft immediately without manual review — best reserved for low-risk responses like out-of-office acknowledgments.

15Inviting your team

Open Settings → Members to manage who has access to a workspace. From there you can:

  • Invite a new teammate by email.
  • Assign a role — Owner, Admin, or Member — controlling how much they can change.
  • Revoke access from someone who has left the team.

Invitees receive an email with a link to sign in and accept the invitation.

16Personal settings

Open Settings → Personal details to:

  • See the email address tied to your account.
  • Change your password.

17Day-to-day workflows

A few of the patterns we expect teams to settle into.

Morning triage of overnight replies

Get through 50 fresh replies in under 30 minutes.

  1. Open All. Newest threads are at the top, already labeled.
  2. Click into anything labeled Interested — the Reply Agent's draft is waiting in the composer.
  3. Read, lightly edit, hit Send.
  4. For anything that needs more thought, click the clock and snooze it to a later time of day.
  5. Archive anything marked Out of Office or Automated Response.

Building a "Follow-Up Day 3" list

Surface threads that are due for a second touch.

  1. Open the filter bar above any list.
  2. Set Labels = NOT [Cold-Leads, Wrong Person, Not Right Now].
  3. Set Reply Since = "more than 3 days, less than 5 days".
  4. Set Last Message From = "Me".
  5. Click Save, name it "Follow-Up Day 3".
  6. Tomorrow morning, the list shows up in your sidebar with the right threads inside.

Onboarding a new client account

New team in EmailBison → ready in Master Inbox in 3 minutes.

  1. Add the team in EmailBison as you normally would.
  2. In Master Inbox, click the workspace switcher → Sync.
  3. The new workspace appears. Switch to it.
  4. Configure AI Labeling and a Reply Agent under Settings — both are blank in a fresh workspace.
  5. Invite the team via Settings → Members.

Closing out the week

Empty inbox by Friday afternoon.

  1. Open All.
  2. For each unresolved thread: reply, snooze to next week, or archive.
  3. Anything in Meetings Booked stays for your records.

18What's coming next

A few features are planned for upcoming phases:

  • Workflow rules — automatic actions like "if labeled Meeting Booked, archive after 7 days" or "if labeled Do Not Contact, push to your CRM".
  • Outbound webhooks — push events (label changes, replies sent) into other tools.
  • Sales intelligence — surface campaign-level insights from your reply data.

The product is delivered in phases; what you have today is the foundation that makes these additions seamless once they're ready.
